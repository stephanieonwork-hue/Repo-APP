L'Oreal Skincare: Moving from 4th to 3rd in SOM

Our recommended approach for the spend-level question from the client team.

The Ask

Can we recommend spending levels to move L'Oreal from 4th to 3rd in skincare SOM?

The client team completed a competitive SOV/SOM audit. L'Oreal currently sits 4th in skincare market share and wants to reach 3rd. They're looking for a spend recommendation to get there.

The Quick Answer

The ESOV approach is directionally right, but limited

The standard ESOV framework (excess share of voice drives share growth) gives us a starting point. If a 10% increase in SOV generates roughly 0.5% growth in SOM, we can reverse-engineer a spend target to close the gap. That math is solid as a back-of-napkin estimate.

The problem: Applying a single industry-average elasticity across the board assumes a linear relationship between spend and share. In reality, channel mix matters enormously. A dollar in a high-elasticity channel (e.g., TV/video, retail media) will drive meaningfully more share than a dollar in a saturated one. The ESOV formula also doesn't account for diminishing returns, competitive response, or timing.

If we deliver a spend number based only on the ESOV rule, we risk either significantly overstating or understating what's required, and the client won't have a plan for how to allocate it.

Our Recommendation

Pair the SOV audit with a measurement-backed growth plan

Rather than delivering a single spend figure, we should frame this as a two-part answer. The audit gives the "where are we" context. The modeling gives the "how do we get there" plan.

ESOV-only approach

Single spend number based on industry-average elasticity.

No guidance on channel allocation or timing.

Assumes competitors hold steady.

Hard to defend in a leadership presentation.

Recommended approach

Brand-specific response curves by channel, built from L'Oreal's own data.

Optimized channel mix showing where each dollar has the most impact.

Scenario-based pathways (accelerated vs. phased) with trade-offs clearly laid out.

Defensible, data-backed story for the client's leadership.

What This Involves

Two core workstreams

Competitive Landscape Audit

Map SOV/SOM across the skincare category by channel and sub-segment. Quantify the gap to 3rd position and identify where competitors are most vulnerable.

Marketing Mix Model + Scenario Planning

Build a skincare-specific MMM to quantify channel-level share elasticity. Use it to model investment scenarios that close the gap, with clear spend, mix, and timeline recommendations.

The audit is likely already underway or near-complete. The MMM and scenario planning would be the incremental workstream we propose to the client as the path to a credible spend recommendation.
